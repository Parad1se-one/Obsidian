# IPD: Boosting Sequential Policy with Imaginary Planning Distillation in Offline Reinforcement Learning

**arXiv:** 2603.xxxxx (March 2026)  
**Authors:** Research on offline RL distillation

## Summary
This paper introduces Imaginary Planning Distillation (IPD), a novel approach for boosting sequential policy learning in offline reinforcement learning settings. The method leverages knowledge distillation to transfer planning capabilities from a teacher policy to a student policy.

## Key Contributions
- **Imaginary Planning Distillation:** A framework that distills planning knowledge from teacher to student policies
- **Offline RL Focus:** Designed specifically for offline reinforcement learning where interaction with environment is limited
- **Sequential Policy Enhancement:** Improves sequential decision-making through distilled planning knowledge

## Method
- Uses a teacher policy with strong planning capabilities
- Distills "imaginary" planning trajectories to student policy
- Enables student to learn complex sequential behaviors without extensive environment interaction

## Applications
- Offline reinforcement learning scenarios
- Settings with limited environment access
- Sequential decision-making tasks

## Notes
- Recent work (March 2026) in the intersection of knowledge distillation and offline RL
- Addresses sample efficiency challenges in offline RL through distillation
