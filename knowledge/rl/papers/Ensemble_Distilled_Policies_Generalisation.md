# How Ensembles of Distilled Policies Improve Generalisation in Reinforcement Learning

**arXiv:** 2510.xxxxx (October 2025)  
**Authors:** Research on ensemble methods and generalization in RL

## Summary
This paper investigates how ensembles of distilled policies can improve generalization in reinforcement learning. The work demonstrates that combining multiple distilled policies leads to better performance on unseen tasks and environments.

## Key Contributions
- **Ensemble Distillation:** Combines multiple distilled policies for improved generalization
- **Generalization Improvement:** Demonstrates enhanced performance on novel tasks
- **Diversity Analysis:** Studies the role of policy diversity in ensemble effectiveness

## Method
- Train multiple teacher policies with different initializations or environments
- Distill each teacher into student policies
- Combine distilled policies into an ensemble
- Leverage diversity for improved generalization
- Evaluate on out-of-distribution tasks and environments

## Applications
- Transfer learning in RL
- Multi-domain reinforcement learning
- Robust policy learning
- Meta-reinforcement learning
- Sim-to-real transfer
